AC electric power is commonly used for lighting and hotel loads on Alaskan fishing vessels. The fuel used to generate AC electricity on a vessel represents 95% of the life-cycle costs of electric motors and lighting equipment. AC powered refrigeration systems are addressed in the refrigeration section. Electricity on a boat is expensive, particularly when the genset is lightly loaded. Simple steps to save fuel

Use LEDs - Replacing lights that are used hundreds of hours per year or more with LEDs typically provides a good return on investment
Use premium efficiency motors (IE3) - A premium efficiency motor can save 20% on energy costs.
Use an inverter - Consider using an inverter for light loads instead of running a genset. A true sine wave inverter is more efficient than a modified sine wave inverter.
Heating alternatives - Consider installing a heat pump or heat exchanger powered by engine coolant instead of electric resistance heating for cabin spaces.
