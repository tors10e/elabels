The amount of power needed to move a vessel through the water increases exponentially with speed. At low speeds, water friction and turbulence are the main sources of resistance. However, as speed increases, wave-making resistance associated with the bow and stern waves increase exponentially until hull speed is reached.

Simple steps to save fuel:

  • Check Exhaust—Exhaust from a well maintained diesel engine is almost invisible. Light blue or white exhaust can be a sign of unburned fuel and reduced engine efficiency.
  • “Right size” propulsion and auxiliary engines.—Bigger is not always better. When re-powering, select an engine which runs efficiently at the average and maximum HP loads of your operation.
  • Take advantage of modern diesel engine technologies—Manufacturers’ data on 20 common diesel engines show significant BSFC differences between two-stroke, four-stroke and modern electronically-governed diesel engines.
