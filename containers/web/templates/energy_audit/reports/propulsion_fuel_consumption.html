Simple steps to save fuel

Reduce speed - Reducing transit speed by 1 knot can reduce fuel consumption by 30%.
Install a fuel flow meter - A fuel flow meter provides the information needed to adjust speed and optimize fuel efficiency in real time based on weather and sea conditions.
Adjust your autopilot - Ensure your autopilot is tuned to minimize yaw and steer the straightest course possible.
Plan the route and timing - Take advantage of tides, currents, and predicted winds to save fuel.
Reduce drag - Keep the hull clean by removing marine growth regularly. Minimize underwater appendages. Polish the propeller when hauled out. Proper hull and propeller maintenance can reduce propulsion costs by 10 to 20% at transit speeds.
Use para vane stabilizers only when necessary - Para vane stabilizers can increase propulsion costs by 60% at transit speeds.
Reduce vessel weight - Loaded or tanked configurations increase fuel consumption at transit speeds by 30%.
